Kanpur City Noise Maps Analysis

Traffic Noise Assessment Across Different Time Periods

Overview

This analysis presents comprehensive noise mapping data for Kanpur city across different time periods (morning, afternoon, and evening). The maps utilize a color-coded system to visualize noise levels at various monitoring locations throughout the city, helping to identify areas with concerning noise pollution levels.

Noise pollution is measured using a Noise Value Index, with lower values (shown in red) indicating quieter zones and higher values (shown in blue) representing higher noise pollution areas.The data collection covers multiple monitoring points labeled L1 through L14.

Noise Level Index Legend

The color coding represents noise values where:

Red: Lowest noise pollution
Orange: Lower noise pollution
Yellow: Moderate noise levels
Light Green: Moderate to high noise pollution
Blue: Highest noise pollution
Noise Maps by Time Period

Morning Noise Mapping

The morning period noise distribution reveals significant variations across monitored locations, with a noise range of 70.9 to 79.3 dB. Hotspots L12, L11, and L13 exhibit elevated noise levels between 76 and 80 dB, primarily due to high traffic. Locations L11 and L13, designated as commercial zones, exceed their prescribed noise limits by 11 dB. Most critically, L12, classified as a silent zone, surpasses its permissible noise limit by approximately 30 dB, which is unacceptable. The quietest area L8, records a noise level of 70.9 dB.

Noise range: 70.9 - 79.3 dB index values

Key Findings and Recommendations

Major Noise Pollution Hotspots

  • Persistent Hotspots: Locations L11, L13, and L12 consistently show high noise pollution levels across all time periods.
  • Time-Specific Patterns: L6 becomes comparitely better during evening hours.
  • Quietest Areas: Location L2,L8,L3 maintain the lowest noise pollution levels across the whole day.

Temporal Patterns

  • Morning: Higher overall noise levels in the North and North eastern parts of the city, likely due to morning commute.
  • Afternoon: Central zones advanced a little higher noise levels compared to morning.
  • Evening: Decreased amount of noise pollution in northern areas and Northeastern areas persistently having high noise values.

Recommendations

  • Implement noise barriers and sound-absorbing infrastructure at identified hotspots (L11, L12, L13).
  • Consider time-specific traffic management strategies, particularly during morning and afternoon peak hours.
  • Evaluate and potentially replicate urban planning aspects of the quieter areas (around L12) in future development projects.
  • Develop targeted noise reduction policies based on the time-of-day patterns observed in this analysis.
Methodology

This noise mapping analysis utilized:

  • Strategic placement of noise monitoring equipment at 14 locations across Kanpur city.
  • Data collection across three time periods: morning, afternoon, and evening.
  • Heat map visualization technique with color-coding based on Noise Value Index.
  • Spatial interpolation to visualize noise propagation between monitoring points.

This project aims to provide urban planners, environmental authorities, and city residents with actionable insights for noise pollution mitigation and improved urban acoustic quality in Kanpur city.
