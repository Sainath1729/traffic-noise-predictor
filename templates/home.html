Introduction

Welcome to our project on vehicular traffic noise analysis and prediction in Kanpur, Uttar Pradesh [the reason for selecting this site is detailed in the About section]. The project aims to assess current noise levels across different city zones and compare them with CPCB standards. Our focus is on vehicular traffic noise, offering insights into the extent of noise pollution and its impact on the local community.

Dataset

Our dataset comprises noise level measurements from 14 locations across Kanpur, collected over six months. Measurements were taken once a month on a fixed day, simultaneously at all locations, ensuring a consistent and comprehensive view of city-wide noise patterns.

At each site, data from two road lanes was recorded during three peak periods—Morning, Afternoon, and Evening—each lasting one hour. Noise levels were measured using an A-weighted scale and reported as equivalent continuous sound levels (Leq) in dB(A).

Methods

Sound Measurement

We used a Class 1 Sound Level Meter (SLM) with an integrated microphone and GPS to measure sound levels and record location data. The SLM has a dynamic range of 21-138 dB(A), where "dB" denotes decibels and "A" represents the human ear's frequency sensitivity. Measurements followed CPCB noise monitoring guidelines.

Speed Detection

Vehicle spot speeds were measured using an HR K-Band Handheld Radar Gun, operating at 24.125 GHz ± 100 MHz, with a speed range of 16-330 km/h in stationary mode and typically 9-334 km/h.

Weather Monitoring

A portable weather station with a handheld display, powered by a Li-NCR18650 battery and equipped with a bird spike, recorded real-time weather parameters such as temperature, humidity, wind speed/direction, and atmospheric pressure.

Traffic Analysis

Data was collected for 12 vehicle types, improving dataset robustness for Indian road conditions. Average traffic volume and speed for each vehicle type were also recorded.

Results

Our predictions and findings indicate that noise levels at many locations, during various peak times, exceeded the permissible limits set by the CPCB. These results underscore the urgent need for implementing noise mitigation strategies in Kanpur.
